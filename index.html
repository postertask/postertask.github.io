Turtles

The largest?

The largest sea turtle is the Leatherback Turtle.

How many species?

The are seven species of marine turtles.

The smallest

The smallest sea turtle is the Loggerhead Turtle.

How many in the Great Barrier Reef?

There are 6 of the worlds 7 sea turtles including the Loggerhead turtle, Green turtle, Hawksbill turtle, Leatherback turtle, Olive Ridley turtle and the Flatback turtle.

The Grean Turtle

A structural adaptation of the Green Turtle are their forelimbs and neck. The forelimbs are modified into long, paddle-like flippers for swimming. It allows the Green Turtle to maintain its speed whilst travelling in the ocean.

Another structural adaptation is the mouth of the turtle, it has no teeth. Their jaws are powerful and shaped like a bird’s beak.

Leatherback Turtle

The world’s largest turtles, Leatherbacks can grow to to around 2m long and weigh up to a massive 900kg – that’s heavier than 11 men!

Green Turtle

The Green turtle can be found nesting along the coastline of more than 80 countries around the world, with the largest nesting populations found in Costa Rica and Australia.

Loggerhead Turtle

Kemp's ridley Turtle

Kemp's ridley sea turtles generally prefer warm waters, but inhabit waters as far north as New Jersey. They migrate to the Gulf of Mexico and Florida, where they often inhabit the waters off Louisiana.

Olive ridley Turtle

The olive ridley turtles weigh between 75-100 pounds (34 - 45 kg) and reach 2-2 ½ feet (roughly .6 m) in length.

Flatback Turtle

Flatbacks are preyed upon by Saltwater crocodiles, the largest reptile on earth. Adult females have been observed being attacked by crocs while attempting to nest.
